Emergency Medicine

Comprehensive Emergency Medicine workflow and revenue cycle management, ensuring compliance, coding accuracy, and efficient billing for high-acuity, time-sensitive care.

Workflow Overview

Arrive → Triage → MSE → Bed → Provider Care → Diagnostics → Re-Eval → Disposition → Document → Code → Bill → Collect

Key Differences from Other Specialties:
  • No scheduled appointments
  • EMTALA-driven care
  • High acuity & time-sensitive workflows
  • Separate professional & facility billing
  • Critical care & trauma services
Patient Arrival & Registration:
  • Arrival via walk-in, ambulance, or transfer
  • Quick registration (name, DOB, chief complaint)
  • EMTALA compliance
  • Insurance capture (post-stabilization)
Triage:
  • Rapid assessment by triage nurse
  • Acuity level assignment (ESI 1–5)
  • Vital signs & initial symptom evaluation
  • Immediate bed assignment for high-acuity patients
Medical Screening Exam (MSE):
  • Performed by qualified medical provider
  • Determine presence of emergency medical condition
  • Stabilization initiation if required
  • Documentation for EMTALA compliance
Patient Placement & Care Initiation:
  • Bed assignment (fast track / main ED / trauma bay)
  • Continuous monitoring
  • Nursing assessment
  • Initiation of protocols (stroke, sepsis, chest pain)
Provider Evaluation:
  • History & focused physical exam
  • Differential diagnosis
  • Orders for labs, imaging, EKG, medications
  • Consultation requests (specialists)
Diagnostic & Therapeutic Services:
  • Point-of-care testing
  • Labs & imaging (CT, MRI, X-ray, US)
  • Procedures (suturing, intubation, splinting)
  • Medication administration
  • Critical care services (if applicable)
Re-Evaluation & Clinical Decision-Making:
  • Monitor patient response
  • Review test results
  • Modify treatment plan
  • Consult disposition teams
Disposition:
  • Discharge with instructions
  • Admission to inpatient unit
  • Observation status
  • Transfer to another facility (if required)
Documentation & Coding:
  • Complete ED provider note
  • ICD-10 diagnosis coding
  • CPT/HCPCS coding (E/M, procedures, critical care)
  • Time-based documentation accuracy
  • Modifier application
Charge Capture:
  • ED visit level assignment (99281–99285)
  • Procedure & supply charges
  • Critical care time capture
  • Facility vs professional charge separation
Claim Submission:
  • Claim scrubbing & edits
  • Electronic submission (837P/837I)
  • Clearinghouse & payer compliance
Payment Posting:
  • ERA/EOB posting
  • Contractual adjustments
  • Patient responsibility posting
  • Observation vs inpatient reconciliation
Denial Management:
  • Medical necessity denials
  • Level-of-care disputes
  • Timely filing issues
  • Appeals & corrections
Patient Billing & Collections:
  • Post-visit insurance billing
  • Patient statements
  • Financial assistance screening
  • Payment plans
Quality, Compliance & Reporting:
  • EMTALA audits
  • Door-to-provider times
  • Left Without Being Seen (LWBS)
  • Throughput & LOS metrics
  • Provider productivity
