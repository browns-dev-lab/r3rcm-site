Internal Medicine / Family Medicine

We manage the complete patient care workflow and revenue cycle for internal medicine and family practice, from scheduling to follow-ups.

Workflow Overview

Schedule → Verify → Check-In → Intake → Provider Visit → Document & Code → Charge → Claim → Payment → Denials → Patient Billing → Follow-Up

Patient Access & Scheduling:
  • Appointment booking (phone, portal, walk-in)
  • New vs established patient identification
  • Demographics capture
  • Insurance information collection
  • Referral intake (if required)
Eligibility & Benefits Verification:
  • Insurance eligibility validation
  • Co-pay, deductible, coinsurance confirmation
  • PCP/referral authorization check
  • Coverage limitations identification
  • Patient financial responsibility estimation
Patient Check-In:
  • Patient arrival confirmation
  • ID & insurance card verification
  • Consent & HIPAA forms
  • Co-pay collection
  • Update demographics & insurance
Clinical Intake (Rooming):
  • Vital signs (BP, temp, pulse, weight)
  • Chief complaint documentation
  • Medication reconciliation
  • Allergy review
  • Medical, family & social history
  • Preventive care gaps review
Provider Evaluation & Care Delivery:
  • History of Present Illness (HPI)
  • Physical examination
  • Assessment & diagnosis
  • Preventive care (annual physicals, screenings)
  • Chronic disease management (Diabetes, HTN, Asthma, etc.)
  • Acute illness treatment
  • Orders: labs, imaging, referrals, medications
Documentation & Coding:
  • Complete SOAP notes
  • ICD-10 diagnosis coding
  • CPT/HCPCS procedure coding
  • Modifier application (if applicable)
  • Documentation compliance review
Charge Capture:
  • Visit level assignment (E/M codes)
  • Procedures & services captured
  • Same-day services posting
  • Charge entry into Practice Management System
Claim Submission:
  • Claim scrubbing & validation
  • Electronic claim submission (837P)
  • Clearinghouse edits resolution
  • Timely filing compliance
Payment Posting:
  • ERA/EOB posting
  • Insurance payments allocation
  • Patient responsibility posting
  • Adjustments & write-offs
  • Denial identification
Denial Management:
  • Denial categorization
  • Root cause analysis
  • Corrected claims submission
  • Appeal preparation & follow-up
  • Trend reporting & prevention
Patient Billing & Collections:
  • Patient statements generation
  • Payment plans
  • Patient calls & follow-ups
  • Online payment processing
  • Bad debt management (if applicable)
Follow-Up & Care Coordination:
  • Lab & test result communication
  • Referral tracking
  • Care gap closure
  • Chronic care follow-ups
  • Transitional care management (TCM)
Reporting & Quality Measures:
  • Revenue performance dashboards
  • A/R aging reports
  • Provider productivity
  • Quality metrics (MIPS)
  • Preventive care compliance
